Title: The Sense of Initiative and Entrepreneurship. The Formation of an Intercultural Competence in Pre-adolescents

Abstract: Among the eight key competences in Europe, the “sense of initiative andentrepreneurship” is the least aimed at the secondary school level and theone young Italians develop much less than their immigrant peers.Our research involves two classes of pre-adolescent students, with a goodnumber of immigrants, which we have divided into working groups. Theywere asked to face reality, authentic tasks and show ability to deal with realproblem solving by adopting cooperative methodologies. The key hypothesisis that intercultural cooperative learning tasks may improve and increasethe processes relevant to the six indicators of competence (idea-actiontranslation, creativity, innovation, risk taking, planning, project management)both for Italian students and non-Italian speakers.
