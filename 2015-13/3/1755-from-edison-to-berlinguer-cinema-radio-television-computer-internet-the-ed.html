Metadata (EN)

Title: From Edison to Berlinguer. Cinema, Radio, Television, Computer, Internet: the educational parabolic trajectory of the 20th century, “age of media” and “age of school”

Abstract: The contribution begins with a brief historical overview that shows how thetechnological and economic revolution of the media, born in the ‘900, hasbeen crossed with the cultural and social life of the school, so as to call it“age of media” but also “age of school”. Then it opens a critical reflectionon the never peaceful relationship between the school culture and the mediaculture, explaining the evolution of communication technologiesthrough three paradigms: the technological one: “education with the media”,the semiotics one: “media literacy”, the pedagogical one: “educationthrough the media.” In the last part it describes the most significant actionto introduce educational technology in the formal educational systems atuniversity and at school, from the 80s till the first big Development Planwhich involved nearly 200,000 teachers.
